Website Success Stories

1

Video Production Company

Challenge: A Milan-based video production studio run by two filmmakers needed a simple, clear way to show both their commercial and creative work in one place.

Solution: We created a straightforward site with easy-to-browse portfolios for ads and short films, with a very simple and customized backend to manage their frequent updates

Result: More people got in touch about projects, and both their commercial and creative work received more attention.

2

Creative Resource Studio

Challenge: A design-and-photography duo sold templates, scene generators, and theme assets, but their site wasn't getting many sales.

Solution: We simplified the site, grouped templates by purpose, and made it easier to buy. We also added clear examples showing how each product could be used.

Result: Sales and conversions increased, and more designers started using their products.

3

Arts, Music, and Wellness Festival Website

Challenge: A festival combining art, music, and yoga events needed a website to clearly present its program, activities, and locations. Their previous online presence was minimal and lacked clear navigation.

Solution: We developed a simple, mobile-friendly site that organized events by date and type, included profiles for artists and instructors, and made it easy for visitors to find schedules and practical information.

Result: Attendees could easily plan their visits, and the festival gained more visibility and engagement from people interested in its mix of cultural and wellness activities.

4

Contemporary Art Gallery Website

Challenge: A contemporary art gallery managing multiple exhibitions throughout the year needed a website to showcase current and past shows, artists, and event details. Their old site was outdated and made it difficult for visitors to find information.

Solution: We designed a clean, easy-to-navigate site with dedicated pages for each exhibition, artist profiles, and a calendar of events. The backend was kept simple so staff could quickly update content for new shows.

Result: Visitors could easily explore exhibitions and artist information, and the gallery saw increased attendance and engagement with its events.
